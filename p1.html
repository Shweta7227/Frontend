African Folktales - A Reflection of Culture

In African folktales, stories reflect the culture where diverse types of animals abound. Animals and birds are often accorded human attributes, making it not uncommon to find animals talking, singing, or demonstrating other human characteristics such as greed, jealousy, honesty, etc.

Animals with Human Attributes

The rich and diverse animal life in Africa is beautifully represented in folktales, with animals and birds displaying human-like qualities. This personification helps to create engaging stories that teach moral lessons and reflect cultural values.

Seasons and Landscape

Many African folktales expose the reader to the unique landforms and climate within various regions of Africa. References are often made to different seasons, such as the 'dry' or 'rainy season,' and the various effects on the surrounding vegetation and animal life.
